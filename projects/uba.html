University Tutoring

During my time as a Teaching Assistant at the University of Buenos Aires, I played an active role in the academic development of students in introductory computing courses. My responsibilities extended beyond technical instruction to include meaningful human engagement and mentorship.

  • Responsibilities: Planning and teaching practical classes, preparing and correcting exams, designing exercises and assignments, conducting feedback sessions, and offering one-on-one student support.

  • Courses: Algorithms and Programming I, Algorithmic Logic (Faculty of Engineering and CBC), UBA

  • This experience significantly enhanced my communication and leadership skills. I learned how to adapt complex technical topics to diverse learning styles, foster group discussion, and support students both academically and personally. It reinforced my belief that teaching is a two-way process—one that cultivates empathy, patience, and a deep understanding of human connection in technical environments.
